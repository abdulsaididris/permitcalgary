Calgary Permit FAQs

Common questions about building and development permits in Calgary, answered by our experts.

Standard residential building permits in Calgary typically take 3 to 8 weeks from the date of a complete submission. Commercial and complex projects may take 8–16 weeks or more. We actively manage your file, respond to all city revisions, and keep you updated throughout the process.

Yes — in Calgary, a building permit is required for most decks, particularly those over 0.6m (2 feet) above grade. The permit ensures your deck meets the Alberta Building Code for structural safety. We handle everything from permit-ready drawings to City approval.

Secondary suites in Calgary typically require both a development permit (land use) and a building permit (construction compliance). They must meet Calgary's Land Use Bylaw 1P2007 and the Alberta Building Code, including fire separation, egress windows, and minimum ceiling heights. We manage both applications from start to finish.

City of Calgary permit fees are calculated based on project value. A simple residential project (e.g., a deck) may have fees starting around $170–$250, while larger renovations and commercial projects scale significantly higher. Our consulting fees are separate and depend on project scope — contact us for a free quote.

A development permit is about land use — it confirms your intended use complies with Calgary's Land Use Bylaw 1P2007. A building permit is about construction — it ensures the work meets the Alberta Building Code for health and safety. Many projects in Calgary require both, and they are applied for through the City of Calgary's permit portal. We handle both seamlessly.
